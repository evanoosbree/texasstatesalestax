Texas sales & use tax snapshot

How Texas Sales Tax Works

Texas imposes a 6.25% state sales and use tax on most taxable retail sales, leases, and rentals of goods and services. Local taxing jurisdictions (cities, counties, special-purpose districts, and transit authorities) can add up to 2.00% in local tax, so the maximum combined rate is 8.25%.

State rate 6.25% statewide base sales & use tax
Local add-ons Cities, counties, transit & special districts up to 2.00% total
Max combined 8.25% total sales & use tax cap on a taxable sale
Use tax Same rates as sales tax when tax wasn’t collected at purchase

Use tax

Texas Use Tax Basics

Texas use tax applies when you buy a taxable item and no Texas sales tax was collected, but you store, use, or consume the item in Texas. The state use tax rate is also 6.25%, and you may owe up to 2.00% in local use tax, matching the local sales tax rate at your location.

Common examples include online or out-of-state purchases where the seller doesn’t charge Texas tax. In those cases, the Texas purchaser is responsible for paying use tax directly to the Comptroller when required.

FAQ

Texas Sales Tax – Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Texas state sales tax rate?

The statewide Texas sales and use tax rate is 6.25% on most taxable sales of goods and services.

What is the maximum total sales tax rate?

Local jurisdictions can impose up to 2.00% combined in local sales and use taxes. When added to the 6.25% state rate, the maximum combined rate on a taxable transaction in Texas is 8.25%.

How do I find my exact local rate?

Go to the official Texas Sales Tax Rate Locator and enter your address. It will show you which taxing entities apply and the correct combined rate.

Are groceries and prescriptions taxed?

Most unprepared food for home consumption and many prescription medicines are exempt from Texas sales tax. Some items (like certain prepared foods, restaurant meals, and non-food items) remain taxable. For detailed exemption lists, refer to the Comptroller’s official publications.
